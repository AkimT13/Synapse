6.3.2 Authority bounds on primary flight surfaces

Any control law implementing commands to primary flight surfaces — elevator, aileron, rudder — must satisfy the properties in this section for Design Assurance Level A (DAL-A) certification. The properties are stated as verification obligations, each of which must be discharged by analysis, test, or both.

↳ Find related code for this paragraph ⌘ ↵

Every commanded surface deflection must be clamped to the physical envelope of the corresponding actuator prior to transmission. The clamp shall execute on every invocation of the control-law function, regardless of prior state, and its limit values shall be traceable to a requirement artifact that identifies the actuator make and model. See ARP4754A §5.2 for the functional-level statement of this requirement.

Verification obligations

  1. The presence of the clamp shall be shown by static analysis over the source of the control-law function.
  2. The clamp limits shall be shown, by requirement trace, to match the actuator envelope documented in the ICD for the target airframe.
  3. The output of the control law shall be shown, by closed-loop simulation, to remain within the clamp bounds for 100% of the verification corpus.

6.3.3 Latency budget

The end-to-end latency from sensor sample to actuator command shall not exceed the frame period specified in the platform timing budget. Synchronous implementations discharge this obligation by construction; asynchronous implementations must present a worst-case analysis.
